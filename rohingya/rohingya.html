Immersive Storytelling of Rohingya Refugee Experience

RAY LC | FABEHA MONIR | ANIKA ULLAH

The displacement of refugees from their natural homes have caused violence and estrangement all over the world, as victims, perpetrators, and hosts jostle for territorial and resource control, to the detriment of victims who live in unbearable conditions outside their homelands. Thereâ€™s often misunderstanding amongst hosts and Western media that see refugees as parasites and destructive agents who hoard valuable resources. Educating both sides of the refugee-host divide have involved programs like UNVR, which created immersive films following the conventional 2D filmmaking approach, portraying static scenes with voiced narration.

A NEW APPROACH

DYNAMIC IMMERSION

Instead of a static camera like traditional films, we move the 360 camera around, letting refugees pass the camera amongst each other, following them when they walk around and play football.

AUTEUR-SHIP

Instead of making the filmmaker disappear in VR narratives, we let ourselves be part of the story, playing and interacting with the refugees to show how the experience lives in real life.

1ST PERSON

Instead of asking them to tell stories in documentary format, we film narratives based on their experience, both positive and negative, and let refugees frame their stories themselves.

EMPOWERMENT

We let refugees tell their own stories using a phone that they can use to record night activities during Ramadan and using a 360 camera that they can take anywhere to show their audience.
